Many of the special effects you see in webpages are achieved through the use of JavaScript. Popular uses for JavaScript include banners, scrolling marquee signs, animations, and changing colors in response to specific mouse movements.
    One of the most popular features of JavaScript is the “mouseOver()” function, which allows you to define specific actions for the browser to take in response to mouse movements. For example, if the user moves the mouse over a specific area or element on a webpage, that element may change shape and color, or start an animation, open a file, etc.
